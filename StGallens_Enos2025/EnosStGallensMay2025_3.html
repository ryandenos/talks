Overview

  1. Many (I mean many!) theories of social and political behavior (explicitly or implicitly) implicate geographic contextual, implicating the environment in which the behavior occurs or the people that fill that environment.
  1. In modern social science, we have amazing measures of individual behavior.
  1. Our measures of context are relatively crude: often relying on aggregate summaries (and sometimes small samples) that bring imprecision and possible bias.

Wide and Deep Geographic Data

Data that is wide and deep overcomes these limitations.

  1. Wide:
  • geographically: 200million American voters
  • temporally: over 10 years of continuous panel (or longer)
  1. Deep:
  • contains socio-politically important behavioral variables
  • connect with other data for more behaviors

## Talk Today

  1. Overview of data
  2. Measurement (studies 1 and 2)
  3. Context as an outcome (study 3)
  4. Context as a cause (study 4)
  5. Connecting with other data (studies 5–10)

  • Each study will offer enough background and results to grasp main point and level critiques.

  • Ultimately, hope to demonstrate fruitful application of approach allowing for new insights on the powerful influence of geographic context on behavior.

### Parting Thoughts… 1. Hope I’ve made the case that this data can significantly enhance our understanding of socio-political behavior.

  1. Limitation: all this research is based in U.S. and based on residential location (rather than other places people can be found).
  1. Unlike many forms of data, say experimental and surveys, big geographic data requires an institution (other than a university) – I fear we might soon see a pulling-back of the availability of this data.
